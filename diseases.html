Common Diseases in Agriculture

Black Spot

Black spot is one of the most common diseases found on roses,can also occur on other ornamental and garden plants.

Powdery Mildew

Powdery mildew is a fungal disease that affects many of our landscape plants, flowers, vegetables and fruits.

Downy Mildew

Downy mildew refers to any of several types of oomycete microbes that are obligate parasites of plants.

Blight

Blight is a plant disease, typically one caused by fungi such as mildews, rusts, and smuts.

Canker

Canker, plant disease, caused by numerous species of fungi and bacteria, that occurs primarily on woody species.

Tar Spot

Tar spot caused by Phyllachora maydis Maubl., an obligate fungus, is a major foliar disease of corn.

Sooty Mold

Sooty mold is a fungal disease that grows on plants and other surfaces covered by honeydew, a sticky substance created by certain insects.

Chlorosis

Chlorosis is a yellowing of leaf tissue due to a lack of chlorophyll. Causes of chlorosis include damaged roots, nutrient deficiencies etc., in the plant.

Gymnosporangium Rusts

Gymnosporangium rusts are a group of closely related diseases caused by fungi that infect both junipers (in particular red cedar) and woody plants in the rose family.

Black Knot

Black Knot, caused by the fungus Apiosporina morbosa, is a very common disease of plants in the genus Prunus.

Elderberry Rust

Elderberry rust is a visually striking fungal disease that affects stems, leaves and flowers of plants in the genus Sambucus (i.e., elderberries).

Fulingo Septica

Dog vomit slime mold (Fuligo septica) might appear to be a fungus, but it's actually not--it's a type of slime mold in the Kingdom Protista, not Fungi.

Peach Leaf Curl

Peach leaf curl, also known as leaf curl, is a disease caused by the fungus Taphrina deformans.

Golden Canker

Golden canker (officially Cryptodiaporthe canker) is a visually distinct and increasingly common disease of alternate-leafed dogwoods such as pagoda dogwood.
